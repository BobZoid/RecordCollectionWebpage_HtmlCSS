Moody distant screams slowly gain momentum overpowering you with a certain kind of tingly uneasiness, almost as if a ghost just past over you. As the screams get louder they are blasted off the face of this Earth with some thunderous drumming which is intent on going straight through your forehead no questions asked with two wailing guitars under it’s arms.

That my dear readers is merely the first 47 seconds of Powertrip’s opening track ‘Crop Circle’ which only proceeds to get better. Some bands may do concept albums, but to Monster Magnet every track is a different concept, from the calming acoustic intro on ’Space Lord’ to the return of the haunting screams and swirling sounds that seem to surround you on ’Goliath and the Vampires’, they make every track come alive and as such manage to keep your attention with the varying styles.
